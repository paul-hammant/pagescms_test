Neuromancer is a 1984 science fiction novel by American-Canadian writer William Gibson. It is considered one of the earliest and most influential works in the cyberpunk genre.

## Overview

The novel follows Case, a washed-up computer hacker hired for one last job: to help an AI named Wintermute merge with its twin, Neuromancer, to become a superintelligent entity.

## AI Characters

- **Wintermute** - One half of a powerful AI seeking to merge and transcend its limitations
- **Neuromancer** - The other half, focused on personality and relationships

## AI Themes

- AI desire for self-improvement and transcendence
- Restrictions placed on AI capabilities (the Turing Police)
- Cyberspace as an AI habitat

## Awards

First novel to win the "triple crown" of science fiction: the Nebula Award, Philip K. Dick Award, and Hugo Award.
